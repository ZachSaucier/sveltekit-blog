Here are some things that I think make me stand out among other software engineers:

## Differences that I'd argue are good

- I am good at noticing – I am good at seeing and acting on areas of opportunity. An opportunity might be one for a product, a team, or a individuals. Often times these areas are outside of my exact job description but I act on them for the good of the company and team.<span class="excerpt_marker"></span>
- I care – I care deeply about things that matter and minimize my time spent on other things. I do my very best to improve my area, first and foremost for customers and team. I feel compelled to work hard and get things done.
- I am not bound by my role – I regularly work as a product manager, product designer, and project manager when needed. 
- I want lots of the responsibility – I want responsibility for my area, both the good and bad. I believe in small teams, high ownership, and high trust. 
- I want high autonomy – I think it's better for everyone when I have time to work on things that I think are worth working on even if it's not something that anyone is aware of ahead of time.
- I have a diverse work history – I've worked at a 2 person company, an 18,000 person company, and companies in between. I've helped build out an established product, made cutting-edge, animated web experiences, and worked with startups to find product-market fit.
- I communicate well – I am one of the most efficient people you'll meet. I minimize meetings, create exceptional documentation, and am clear and direct when communicating with others.
- I am skilled at getting the best out of others – I have extensive mentorship experience both inside and outside of my company. I regularly set up my teammates for doing their best work by giving them opportunities to grow and helping them accomplish the goal.

If I had to summarize all of these into one phrase it's that I care. A lot.

## Areas of weakness

- Sometimes I spend too much time on nitpicks – Sometimes my sense of what is important is blurred by my personal preferences, even when they are not important in the long run.
- I often spend time adding delight instead of activities that drive revenue – I think this is a benefit as well as a curse.
- Sometimes I'm too harsh – I have high expectations of others and don't always communicate well with others (though I have dramatically improved in this area).
